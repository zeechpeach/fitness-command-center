Lifting program 4 days
Shoulders + abs — width and capped shoulder look; prioritize lateral delts.
  • Seated DB lateral raise 4 × 12–15 Slow eccentric, 3 sec down
  • Cable lateral raise 3 × 15–20 Constant tension beats dumbbells here
  • Seated DB overhead press 3 × 10–12 Compound anchor
  • Rear delt cable fly 3 × 15–20 Face the cable, arms wide
  • Cable crunch 4 × 15–20 Weighted abs build faster
  • Hanging leg raise 3 × 12–15 Full hip flexion at top
Upper chest + arms — clavicular head for chest fullness without lower pec overhang.
⚠ More taxing — avoid the day before a high-intensity fencing session. Never do day B before a big fencing day; pressing muscles stabilize the sword arm.
  • Incline barbell press 4 × 6–10 30–45° angle, primary mover
  • Incline DB fly 3 × 12–15 Stretch at bottom, don't lock out
  • Cable crossover (high to low) 3 × 15 Emphasizes upper chest insertion
  • EZ-bar curl 4 × 10–12 Supinated grip, full ROM
  • Incline DB curl 3 × 12 Stretch position hits long head of bicep
  • Overhead tricep extension 4 × 10–12 Long head — adds the horseshoe
Lats + abs — wide lats for V-taper foundation.
  • Wide-grip pull-up 4 × 6–10 Weighted if bodyweight is easy
  • Straight-arm cable pulldown 3 × 12–15 Isolates lat without bicep fatigue
  • Single-arm DB row 3 × 10–12 Full stretch at bottom
  • Seated cable row (wide grip) 3 × 12 Drive elbows back and wide
  • Ab wheel rollout 4 × 10–12 Best weighted abs exercise
  • Side plank hip raise 3 × 12/side Obliques sharpen the V shape
Arms + shoulder detail — direct arm stimulus plus finishing shoulder detail.
⚠ More taxing — give yourself a day before a high-intensity fencing session.
  • Barbell curl 4 × 8–10 Heavy, bicep peak
  • Hammer curl 3 × 12 Brachialis thickness
  • Cable curl (low) 3 × 15 Pump finisher, peak contraction
  • Close-grip bench press 4 × 8–10 Tricep mass builder
  • Tricep pushdown (rope) 3 × 15 Lateral head, adds width
  • Face pull 3 × 20 Rear delt + rotator cuff — shoulder health given fencing volume
Scheduling guidance
  • ✓ Days A and C are lower fatigue — fine before or after fencing.
  • ◎ Days B and D are more taxing — allow a day before a high-intensity fencing session.
  • ⚠ Never do day B the day before a big fencing day. Pressing muscles stabilize the sword arm.
  • ↺ No leg work in this program. All sessions 45–55 min.
Nutrition baseline
Breakfast: 3 hardboiled eggs + 2 string cheese
Lunch/dinner: 4–6 oz lean meat + grilled veggies + quinoa or couscous
Snacks: Protein shake (41 g) or Greek yogurt (30 g) + protein bar (20 g)
Carbs concentrated around training sessions. Protein 155–172 g.
